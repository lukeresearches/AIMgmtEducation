Course Design Process

Strategic approach to curriculum development

1. Clarify Vision & Constraints

Meet with FoM leadership to define goals, timelines, and how courses anchor future AI offerings

2. Draft High-Level Outline

Sketch shared outline with modules, core concepts, and assessment approach

3. Map to Existing Curriculum

Align draft outline with current objectives, assignments, and pathways

4. Ground in Evidence

Refresh literature reviews and consult employers to refine outcomes

5. Design Modular Blocks

Create reusable modules that support both undergrad and MBA levels

6. Pilot & Refine

Test selected modules, collect AI-readiness data and faculty feedback

7. Complete Detailed Syllabus

Translate framework into week-by-week syllabus with topics, readings, and labs

8. Prepare for Scale

Package teaching guides, rubrics, and tech setup for other instructors

Six-Week Curriculum

Progressive journey from foundations to application

Week 1

AI Disruption & Literacy Foundations

AI overview, generative vs. predictive AI, basic model logic, prompting and context, AI innovation, ethics (bias, privacy, climate impacts).

Application: Students complete an "AI impact scan" on an industry/function they care about and write a brief on risks, opportunities, and open questions.
Week 2

AI as a Learning Partner & Mindset

AI tools for learning (NotebookLM, ChatGPT), prompting for learning, human in the loop, academic integrity, critical reflection on outputs.

Application: Test an AI-enhanced study workflow, interview a peer about AI use, and revise personal "AI use guidelines."
Week 3

AI Value Propositions

How AI creates value in organizations, automating decisions and workflows, AI-native and AI-disrupted firms, linking capabilities to business models.

Application: Teams draft and critique an AI-enabled value proposition for a real management problem.
Week 4

AI Skills: Research & Data Analysis

AI-assisted secondary research, summarizing reports, qualitative data analysis, basics of AI-assisted coding or no-code tools.

Application: Use AI to synthesize research and code qualitative data into themes, producing a management memo.
Week 5

AI in Business, Finance & Entrepreneurship

Cross-functional use cases in marketing, operations, HR, and finance. AI in forecasting and decision support, AI-enabled startups.

Application: Case-based lab analyzing how AI reshapes business models and proposing improvements.
Week 6

AI in Organizations: Leadership & Collaboration

AI-enabled workflows and roles, collaborating with AI, change management, equity and inclusion in AI adoption.

Application: Teams redesign a workflow to integrate AI responsibly and present a plan highlighting benefits, risks, and leadership actions.
